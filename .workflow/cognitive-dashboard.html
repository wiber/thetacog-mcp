How This Works

Each hour has a cognitive theme. Your terminal changes not just your tools -- it changes your identity. When you open Cursor at 2am, you're not just coding -- you're in the Laboratory, where breaking things safely is expected.

Ollama knows the schedule. When it prompts you at 11am in Terminal, it's not generic -- it's Voice mode during social peak hours. It knows to ask about content amplification, not infrastructure.

Work flows through the day. Vault proves theorems at 4am → Voice makes them viral at noon → Operator closes deals at 5pm → Architect maps patterns at 9am → Builder ships at 3pm → New questions return to Vault. It's a flywheel.

The music is the physics. Each hour's track is a Key-Lock for that specific coordinate in time. Playing a Thud Check when your system drifts re-syncs your neural state (S) with the mechanical beat (P) anchored by your hardware (H). S=P=H made audible.

Voice Question Pattern (Live)

The system initiates. Every 5 minutes, the VoiceBroadcaster reads the mailbox (pending tasks, queue depths, schedule block) and generates a voice note via LLM. It predicts your answer and asks for nuance.

You respond by voice. Record a voice memo reply in Discord. The bot auto-executes immediately -- no reaction needed for authorized users. Your nuance redirects the predicted action.

The loop: Read mailbox → LLM predicts answer → TTS voice note → Discord → You reply → Auto-execute → Next task

Schedule tag format: BLOCK NAME | Time CST

Fallback: When Ollama is saturated, broadcasts use a deterministic template with schedule context instead of skipping silently.
