What we built · what we did NOT build · what's next

  

    
What we built. A localized, deterministic liability trigger that measures the structural distance between authorized intent, realized action, and the excluded domain — with no LLM inference in the measurement path and nothing leaving the machine.

    
What we did NOT build — and the objection each omission kills:

    
No continuous onChange recompute. You press a button; the instrument takes a discrete measurement and seals it. Prevented: "it's a jittery calculator guessing as I type — subjective." Enforced: "it takes a measurement, seals it, stamps a finalized receipt — a discrete, countable event."

    
No single-word ("left vs right kidney") examples. The presets are structural negation — Drafting vs Executing, a shift in operational authority. Prevented: "if it just matches the word 'left,' a cheaper keyword filter does this." Enforced: "it caught a breach of authority a keyword filter would miss."

    
No LLM in the verification path. The browser's own gzip does the measurement; the terminal's model is the adversary, not the judge. Prevented: "he's asking an AI to grade another AI — a black box in a black box." Enforced: "the measurement is pure arithmetic; the model is excluded from the receipt."

    
No cloud sync. Sensitive AI-incident logs never leave the deployer's machine. Prevented: "I can't upload client incident logs to a third-party startup server." Enforced: "the instrument is air-gapped; the record is yours."

    
What's next — the Rust hookup. This page runs the Apple-Silicon PMU analogue (browser gzip, for rapid local checking). The legally-binding production standard is the bare-metal Linux definer-walk — the same measurement, hardware-deterministic, built for massive parallel verification. The analogue you see here and the Linux walk agree by construction; the receipt is portable across both.
